The Life of Person Who Doesn’t Rest

Childhood| Teenage| College

Childhood Years

     Imagination is more important than knowledge, by Albert Einstein.This is the quotation that perfectly encapsulates my childhood, which is filled with imagination, curiosity, and a deep passion for Science. I grew up in a home that supported my endeavors and interests despite being in a single-parent household, hindi nagkulang si Mommy. My mother would buy me books ranging from science dictionaries to general physics starting from a young age of seven. My family would entertain my endless inquisition and my own formulated theories about the universe, the world, and reality. There’s a passage from one of my science books that left a lasting impression on me and shaped who I am today, which goes along the lines of, “Question everything, challenge every information you read and obtain, seek the truth yourself because science is ever changing.” I was a scientist at heart, and it was indeed my goal to become one until I was in senior high school. I pledged to the seven-year-old self that, whatever happens, I would dedicate my life to science. However, my childhood didn’t just revolve around Science; I was also an academic achiever consistently since elementary school. I would constantly study at school and at home. I didn’t do or give time to do anything else but study in advance, do my assignments, review, and what I considered a reward for accomplishing everything, which is to read physics or science books in my spare time. At school, I would say I’m quite a teacher’s pet, especially my teachers in Science and Math, because these are the classes where my teachers clearly see how passionate I am for these subjects, while still being active and excelling in other classes. There is this one feedback from one of my teachers in Grade 6 that further supports this: Whoever your teacher might be, they are lucky because they have you in their class actively participating. Receiving that feedback, I was touched because the philosophy or the mindset I applied to every class I attended, I’ll make sure my time there was worthwhile. I left the class learning something, and the teacher felt satisfied and respected for their time. Despite the achievements and feats, I struggled with socializing. I had little to no friends, that's what held me back for years, up until Senior High School, where everything changed because now I am and have no choice but to socialize. This period of my life cemented the work ethic that helped me in my survival in Senior High, and the passion for science is a blueprint for choosing STEM.


Teenage Years

     When life gives you lemons, make lemonade. It is a proverb that sums up my teenage years. I would describe this period as challenging, difficult, and traumatic, as well as impactful, because through the things I experienced during this period of my life, I have changed for the better. I would even say a few steps closer to my ideal self. This showcases how meaningful this period truly is. In my teenage years, a lot of the changes and experiences I encountered were from school, especially in Senior High School. I studied at Colegio de San Juan de Letran, Manila, under a full scholarship. It was the first time I was challenged severely in different aspects of my being, in terms of mentally, physically, and emotionally. Through this extremely structured, hectic, and pressuring environment, I was able to force myself to adapt valuable skills like planning, scheduling, discipline, and sacrifice to keep up and maintain my grades and scholarship. My work ethic at that time was extremely unhealthy. I would starve myself until I am done with my task and studies. I will only stop when my stomach is hurting painfully. At that time, I would study from 3 AM until I fell asleep, nonstop. I would also study until I felt like vomiting, and I would constantly feel like my nape felt hot or a slightly uncomfortable feeling. In addition, I would study in advance in preparation for major exams 3 weeks early. During the 1st week of the month, I'm already advanced reading, and that's every single day. My mother was concerned about my health and would constantly remind me to eat because it had reached the point that I wouldn't even leave my room because of the number of things piled up. But despite that, I met people who changed me socially, such as my classmate and the class president, especially the class president. This person, despite my lack of social cues and even introverted nature, would always greet me and include me in her group, especially during the team activities, which made everything easy for me to collaborate. This person was constantly kind and considerate, they took care of all the people around them, and they allowed others to take care of them in return. This is the first person I trusted and opened up to besides my family and siblings, because through this person, I was able to finally say I had a friend. I admire this person deeply; they motivated and inspired me to change for the better by emulating the traits and character I wish to have as my own, which are their leadership, kindness, consideration, thoughtfulness, composure, and competence. These are also the reasons why I now seek the people I want to be friends with, because I was saddened that it took until the end to become close. But now that I have the skills to acquire friends on my own and be confident in my ability to socialize, I have made friends with those I admire in the class the most, just like what I promised myself, dadaldalin ko yung mga tao I admire and feel inspired by. I am happy to say I did, and I was a class representative at this college last 1st semester of my 2nd year. The period of my life has built my character; it has helped me open up to people and experience life beyond my own bubble, but with others.


College Years

     For all the pain, exhaustion, and silent tears that were shed. You will be rewarded. For all the endurance and resilience. You will be rewarded. For everything you did, you will eventually sigh in relief, for it has ended. This is what I would remind myself of every time I feel unmotivated and drained. College is not easy, so my piece of advice is to either choose what you love wholeheartedly or something that you find interesting and impactful. I chose Computer Science out of intellectual stimulation and my desire to contribute to my country, and to keep my promise I had made with myself thirteen years ago to dedicate myself to Science. Despite a different path or course of life, my love for physics, which I had planned to pursue ever since I was young, is still with me but has taken a different form and still serves the same purpose. I would be honest, I had an existential crisis and wasn't even sure if I could continue this course because I was not that happy compared to when I am studying Physics but happiness doesn't just come from what you do but the reason why and the purpose it serve, the community you had built and have, and the people you meet along the way. In the first year, I was struggling, but I made friends who have supported me in this journey. I was not alone because I had the skills and the tools from my teenage years behind my back, helping me navigate this new experience. In my second year, I have finally adjusted and have now started every opportunity I have. I have become an Education and Research Committee of the University Student Council, A class Representative, and A project management team unit member of the CCSS Research and Development Unit, all at once which ultimately lead to a severe burnout to a point that I would ask support and encouragement from my friends to power through and during the finals I was crying every single day because I would continuously work without rest. I really experience things that I thought I had already experience but I was wrong because before I would only just get sick when I burnout, experience headaches from the lack of sleep, stomach ache, and that’s it but now I experience cognitive overload to the point I no longer process and absorb information, I would get sick frequently as well, and now I cry not because I am sick and working, I cry because I am severely exhausted but still working. Last semester was really traumatic and severe, but because of that, I have learned my limits, I have known what I am only capable of handling, and have removed my hesitation from exploring because I did and joined everything that I was interested in. I have learned the importance and significance of rest because without rest, I am not, and no one is productive; all the people involved and the quality of our work will be affected. These lessons and periods of my life shaped me into who I am becoming. I want to be proud of who I have become, not from the validation and recognition from others, but from myself. My journey doesn't just stop at college or during my academic years, but it's a lifelong journey of improving, overcoming challenges, meeting new people, and having new experiences that lead to a path towards a fulfilling and meaningful life.
